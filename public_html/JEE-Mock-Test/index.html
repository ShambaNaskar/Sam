Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) about JEE


1. What is the JEE exam?

The JEE (Joint Entrance Examination) is a national-level engineering entrance exam in India conducted for admission to various undergraduate engineering programs at prestigious institutions like IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other renowned engineering colleges.


2. What are the two main exams under JEE?

The two main exams conducted under JEE are:
- JEE Main: Entry criterion for various undergraduate engineering programs at NITs, IIITs, and other participating institutions.
- JEE Advanced: Gateway for admission to IITs for undergraduate courses in engineering, science, or architecture.


3. What subjects are included in the JEE syllabus?

The JEE syllabus typically includes subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, covering various topics from these subjects studied in classes 11th and 12th.


4. Who is eligible to appear for JEE exams?

Students who have completed their 10+2 or equivalent exam from a recognized board with the required subjects (typically Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) are eligible to appear for the JEE exams.


5. How can I prepare for JEE exams effectively?

Effective preparation for JEE exams involves a comprehensive study plan, practicing previous years' question papers, taking mock tests, focusing on conceptual understanding, and seeking guidance from expert resources like coaching institutes or online study materials.


6. Is there any negative marking in JEE exams?

Yes, both JEE Main and JEE Advanced have a provision for negative marking. For every incorrect answer, a certain number of marks are deducted from the total score.


7. When are the JEE exams typically conducted?

The JEE Main exam is usually conducted multiple times a year, while JEE Advanced is conducted once a year. The specific dates for these exams can vary and are announced by the conducting authorities.


8. What is the mode of the JEE exams?

The JEE Main exam is usually conducted in computer-based mode (online), while JEE Advanced is also computer-based but includes a mix of multiple-choice and numerical answer-type questions.

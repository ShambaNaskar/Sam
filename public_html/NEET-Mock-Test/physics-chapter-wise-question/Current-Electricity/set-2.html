3.

The internal resistance of a 2.1 V cell which gives a current of 0.2 A through a resistance of 10 Ω is:

A.

1.0 Ω

B.
0.2 Ω
C.
0.5 Ω
D.
0.8 Ω
Ans. A B C D
Correct answer: Option C
To find the internal resistance (r) of the cell, we can use Ohm's law. Ohm's law states:
V = IR
Where: V is the voltage (2.1 V for the cell).
I is the current (0.2 A through the external resistance).
R is the total resistance in the circuit, which is the sum of the internal resistance (r)
and the external resistance (10 Ω).
So, we have:
2.1 V = (0.2 A) × (r + 10 Ω)
Now, we can solve for the internal resistance (r):
2.1 V = 0.2 A × r + 0.2 A × 10 Ω
2.1 V = 0.2 A × r + 2 V
Subtract 2 V from both sides:
0.1 V = 0.2 A × r
Now, divide both sides by 0.2 A to find r:
r = (0.1 V) / (0.2 A)
r = 0.5 Ω
So, the internal resistance of the cell is 0.5 Ω, which corresponds to option (C) 0.5 Ω.

7.

A 100-W bulb and a 25-W bulb are designed for the same voltage. They have filaments of the same length and material. The ratio of the diameter of the 100-W bulb to that of the 25-W bulb is

A.

4:1

B.
2:1
C.
1:√2
D.
√2:1
Ans. A B C D
Correct answer: Option B
We know that the power of a bulb is related to the area of its filament. The power is directly proportional to the surface area of the filament. The surface area of the filament is proportional to the square of its diameter. So, if we have a 100-W bulb and a 25-W bulb with filaments of the same material and length, the ratio of their diameters should be proportional to the square root of the power ratio (100 W / 25 W). √(100/25) = √4 = 2 Therefore, the correct answer is (B) 2:1. The ratio of the diameter of the 100-W bulb to that of the 25-W bulb is 2:1.

8.

When a wire of uniform cross-section a, length l and resistance R is bent into a complete circle, resistance between two of diametrically opposite points will be

A.

R/4

B.
R/8
C.
4R
D.
R/2
Ans. A B C D
Correct answer: Option A
The circumference has resistance of R. so half of the circumference has a resistance of r/2 and two of these segments are in parallel, we can calculate the equivalent resistance as follows:
Each half of the circumference has a resistance of r/2.
When two of these segments are in parallel, the equivalent resistance (Rp) is given by:
1 / Rp = 1 / (r/2) + 1 / (r/2)
This simplifies to:
1 / Rp = 2 / (r/2)
Now, invert both sides of the equation to find Rp:
Rp = (r/2) / 2
Rp = r / 4
So, the equivalent resistance between two diametrically opposite points in the circular wire is r/4. The correct answer is therefore (A) R/4.

9.

The effective resistance of a parallel connection that consists of four wires of equal length, equal area of cross-section and same material is 0.25 Ω. What will be the effective resistance if they are connected in series? NEET 2021

A.

4Ω

B.
0.25Ω
C.
0.5Ω
D.
1Ω
Ans. A B C D
Correct answer: Option A
When four wires of equal length, equal area of cross-section, and the same material are connected in parallel, their effective resistance (Rp) is calculated as:
1/Rp = 1/R + 1/R + 1/R + 1/R
Since all four wires are identical:
1/Rp = 4/R
Solving for Rp, we get:
Rp = R/4
So, when connected in parallel, the effective resistance is one-fourth of the resistance of a single wire.
Given that Rp is 0.25Ω and the resistance of each wire (R) is 1Ω:
Rp = 0.25Ω
Now, for the effective resistance when these wires are connected in series (Rs):
Rs = 4×R = 4×1Ω = 4Ω
Therefore, the correct answer is (a) 4Ω for the effective resistance when these wires are connected in series.

10.

A cell has an emf 1.5 V. When connected across an external resistance of 2 Ω, the terminal potential difference falls to 1.0 V. The internal resistance of the cell is

A.

1.5 Ω

B.
2 Ω
C.
0.5 Ω
D.
1.0 Ω
Ans. A B C D
Correct answer: Option D
Given:
EMF of the cell (E) = 1.5 V
Terminal potential difference (Vt) when connected to an external resistance (Re) of 2 Ω = 1.0 V
We can use the equation for the terminal potential difference of a cell in a circuit with
internal resistance (r):
Vt = E - Ir
Where:
Vt is the terminal potential difference.
E is the electromotive force (EMF) of the cell.
I is the current flowing through the circuit.
r is the internal resistance of the cell.
We need to find the internal resistance (r). We can rearrange the equation to solve for r:
r = (E - Vt) / I
First, let's find the current (I) using Ohm's law:
I = Vt / Re
I = 1.0 V / 2 Ω
I = 0.5 A
Now, we can calculate r:
r = (1.5 V - 1.0 V) / 0.5 A
r = 0.5 V / 0.5 A
r = 1 Ω

So, the correct answer is (D) 1.0 Ω. The internal resistance of the cell is 1.0 ohm.

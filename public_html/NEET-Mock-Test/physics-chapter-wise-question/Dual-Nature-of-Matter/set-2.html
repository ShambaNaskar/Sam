A.

Four times

B.
One-fourth
C.
Zero
D.

Correct answer: Option C
when the frequency of the incident light is halved to 1.5 times the threshold frequency (ν0), it becomes less than the threshold frequency, and no photoelectric effect takes place. In this case, no photoelectrons would be emitted, and the photoelectric current becomes zero.

9.

Photoelectric emission occurs only when the incident light has more than a certain minimum: AIPMT 2011

A.

power

B.
wavelength
C.
intensity
D.
frequency
Ans. A B C D
Correct answer: Option D
Photoelectric emission occurs only when the incident light has more than a certain minimum frequency (d). This minimum frequency is known as the "threshold frequency." Below this threshold frequency, no photoelectric effect occurs, regardless of the power, wavelength, or intensity of the incident light. When the incident light frequency exceeds the threshold, electrons can be emitted from the material's surface.
